About me

I received by B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics in 2023 from UC Berkeley. Current Research Project

I am working with Professor Satish Rao on examining graph decomposition as a method for improving fast algorithms. Many recent advances in runtime for problems (e.g. the recent almost-linear time advance for the max-flow problem) rely on graph decomposition as a subroutine as well as having dynamic data structures robust to adversial randomness. I am working on efficient algorithms for the cut conductance problem, and examining it using the cut matching paradigm. I am also looking at the semidefinite programming approach to see if it provides intuition on some bounds.

I am also currently attending the Data Structures and Optimization for Fast Algorithms workshop at the Simons Institute for Fall 2023. I hope to gain more knowledge and experience attending talks and communicating with leading researchers in the field.
